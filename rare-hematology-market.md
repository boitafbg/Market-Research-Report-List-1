<p><ul><li>Recombinant Factors</li><li>Plasma Derived Factors</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The rare hematology market comprises therapies aimed at treating blood disorders, primarily using two types of products: recombinant factors and plasma-derived factors. Recombinant factors are engineered proteins synthesized in laboratories that replicate the function of naturally occurring coagulation factors, offering targeted treatment for conditions like hemophilia. In contrast, plasma-derived factors are derived from human plasma and contain proteins essential for blood clotting. Both types play crucial roles in managing rare hematological conditions, with unique advantages and limitations in safety and efficacy.</p></p>
