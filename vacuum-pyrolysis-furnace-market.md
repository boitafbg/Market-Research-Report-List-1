<p><ul><li>Vertical</li><li>Horizontal</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Vacuum pyrolysis furnaces are classified into vertical and horizontal types based on their design and operation. Vertical furnaces have a cylindrical structure that allows materials to be loaded from the top, promoting efficient processing and facilitating space optimization. In contrast, horizontal furnaces feature a long, tubular design where materials are introduced from one end, supporting continuous operation and homogeneous heating. Both types are utilized for decomposing organic materials under controlled conditions to produce bio-oils, gases, and char while minimizing environmental impact.</p></p>
