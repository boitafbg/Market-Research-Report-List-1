<p><p>Direct Renin Inhibitors (DRIs) are a class of antihypertensive drugs that work by directly inhibiting the activity of renin, an enzyme that is crucial in the regulation of blood pressure. By blocking renin, these inhibitors reduce the production of angiotensin I, leading to lower levels of angiotensin II, which subsequently reduces vasoconstriction and aldosterone secretion, leading to decreased blood pressure.</p><p>The Direct Renin Inhibitors Market is anticipated to experience significant growth, driven by increasing prevalence of hypertension and cardiovascular diseases worldwide.
