<p><p>Independent microgrid markets are primarily classified into DC microgrids and AC microgrids, each serving distinct applications. DC microgrids are commonly used for renewable energy integration, electric vehicle charging, and data centers, emphasizing efficiency and reduced conversion losses. In contrast, AC microgrids are favored for their compatibility with existing power grids, supporting conventional loads and enabling seamless interconnections. Both types promote energy independence, sustainability, and resilience by allowing localized control of energy resources while addressing unique operational needs.</p></p>
