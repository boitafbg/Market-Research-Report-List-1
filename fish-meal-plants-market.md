<p><p>The fish meal plants market is primarily segmented into two processing types: wet processing and dry processing. Wet processing involves cooking fish in water, which helps extract oils and nutrients, resulting in high-quality meal suitable for aquaculture. In contrast, dry processing entails grinding raw fish into a powder after drying it, yielding a lower moisture content meal that is cost-effective and has a longer shelf life. Both methods serve various applications in animal feed and nutrition industries.</p></p>
