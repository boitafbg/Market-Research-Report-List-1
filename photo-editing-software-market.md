<p><p>The photo editing software market is primarily divided into two types: RAW editing software and non-RAW editing software. RAW editing software focuses on processing and enhancing RAW image files directly from cameras, offering greater control over exposure, color balance, and detail. It caters to professional photographers and enthusiasts who require high-quality results. In contrast, non-RAW editing software typically works with standard image formats like JPEG and TIFF, providing more accessible tools for casual users looking to apply basic edits and filters.</p></p>
