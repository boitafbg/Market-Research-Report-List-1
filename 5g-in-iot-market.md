<p><p>In the IoT market, 5G technology is categorized into two primary architectures: Standalone (SA) and Non-Standalone (NSA). The 5G NR Standalone Architecture operates independently, utilizing 5G core infrastructure to deliver enhanced features like lower latency and higher capacity, making it ideal for advanced IoT applications. In contrast, the 5G NR Non-Standalone Architecture enhances existing 4G networks by integrating 5G components, allowing for quicker deployment and interim compatibility, particularly useful for current IoT deployments still reliant on 4G technology.</p></p>
